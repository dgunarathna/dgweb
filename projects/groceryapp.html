UX Design

GroceryGo

My Role

UX Design

Duration

10 Days

Year

2022

Problem

Create a tool that can order the pasta sauce from a local small business.

You plan to cook pasta tonight and realize that you don’t have pasta sauce at home. You don’t have enough time to go to the supermarket, so you want to get it delivered to your home. Create a high-fidelity user flow that will show how you order the pasta sauce from a local small business

First I thought this is talking about me or someone like me, who just want a pasta sauce within a few hours to get delivered. If I'm the user I would like to order via over-the-phone because it solves the many problems within the main problem instantly. eg -

When buyer orders something some sellers put different flavors/manufacturers without asking from the buyer. And sometimes sellers delivered larger sizes instead of small ones that the buyer ordered. Orders never get delivered on time.

When it ordering via over the phone as a buyer I can instantly choose & order what I really want like flavor/manufacture/size etc. When selecting a better solution from other solutions I choose mobile app instead of website because based on the qualitative research data I found that uses wants to track their order and get notify when it's get ready to deliver.

Solution

Persona

First, I created the personas which are a fictional character that represents a selection of this real audience and their behaviors to help keep your efforts user-centered before proceeding to ideation.

User Flows

Based on user needs, I mapped out the user flow chart. It gives me an idea about how to organize the content and how many interactions will take to complete the task.

Information Architecture

Then I organized content to take an idea about the structure and prioritizing the information of the content that helps users to find useful information and solve their problems.

Wireframes

And then I created a wireframe to structuralized and prioritizing the information and content using computer-based tools.

Prototype

Finally, I design the enclosure version of the final product that mostly concisely about the visual style guides of the product.

What I learned

Throughout the design process, I learned the importance of empathizing the users and creating solutions by focusing on their pain points and needs.

By conducting user testing, I learned about the users much more and their capabilities. The first solution that I came for this problem doesn't fill the all user needs in the first place, but after a few iterations, I could able to find the best solution from among all other solutions that I imagine.
